Unit 1 Two Words to Avoid, Two to Remember（例） - wolai 笔记
2021.02.24

课前

General questions

  1. What is the writing about? Information
  2. What kind of writing is it? 体裁
  3. What is/are most impressive for you? Feedback
  4. Can you produce another title for the text?
  5. What is the structure of the text?
作者介绍：Arthur Gordon，教材没有介绍（影响一般）
Two words指什么？'if only' 'next time'. 这两个词如何关联？
'if only'，would，虚拟的情况；'next time'，将来时，语言功能上，表达plan，也可以表达说话者的原因
两种生活：态度Regreting form the past Vs. Keep forward to the future

词汇

读课文以前对词有一个深刻的认识，语音、形态以及词义知识
  1. insight：in-sight洞见，英语中没有pre-sight，提前不会有什么深刻的认知
  2. chew the cud：品一品
  3. gnome：[noʊm]两个音在一起搭配，爆破音牺牲；词义，文中用来描述人精气神好
  4. berate：blame，意思相近，搭配相同；张三责备李四，介词for
  5. a perverse streak：perverse, stubborn
  6. click：拟声词，钟表的时针滴答滴答地走
  7. immortality：[.ɪmɔr'tæləti] 重音
理解文中词汇
  1. miscalculation：calculation数数，mis-数错了
  2. strike out：离开，出去
  3. in full stride：英语中的习语

文本分析

Genre：narrative（小说）
1. Nothing in life is more exciting and rewarding than the sudden flash of insight that leaves you a changed person not only changed, but changed for the better. Such moments are rare, certainly, certainly, but they come to all of us. Sometimes from a book, a sermon, a line of poetry. Sometimes from a friend……
Nothing in life is more exciting and rewarding than英语中比较级的否定表示最高级
the sudden flash of insight顿悟，flash在英语里是unit noun，partitive表量词语。知道一群明星、暴徒的“群”，a gang of暴徒
表示紧张，用什么量词
rewarding：得到回报，翻译
leaves you a changed person：为什么这么说？that changes you不是更简洁吗？体会一下差异。经过消化，改变
Sometimes from. Sometimes from a friend：这不是一个完整的句子，文中表示顿悟的来源，sermon；为什么两个分开了，用and连接可不可以？后文相关
2. That wintry afternoon in Manhattan, waiting in the little French restaurant, I was feeling frustrated and depressed. Because of several miscalculations on my part, a project of considerable importance in my life had fallen through. Even the prospect of seeing a dear friend (the Old Man, as I privately and affectionately thought of him) failed to cheer me as it usually did. I sat there frowning at the checkered table-cloth, chewing the bitter cud of hindsight.
wintry和winter的区别：一年四季哪几个可以构成形容词，rain，rainy。作定语时，前者是冬季（寒冷）的下午，后者是冬日的午后
frustrated：困惑，不知所措
第一句翻译的时候，把waiting当成主句会不会更好，waiting怎么翻
Because of：对前文的解释，fall through失败，miscalculation，计划，设计出了错
prospect：前景，朝前，马上要见老朋友了，也没有让我高兴起来
chewing the bitter cud of hindsight：反复想着，苦涩，hindsight怎么翻译？
2021.02.26
标题如果用祈使句，就太过强势
❓Can you use one sentence to repeat the idea of the first paragraph?
概要
mental state: frustrated and depressed
miscalculation是原因；seeing a dear friend failed是这种心情的例证；sat there frowning at是动作表现
I sat there句翻译：我坐在那，对着方格桌布，眉头紧锁，脑海里盘旋着之前发生的事情，满心懊悔。
3. He came across the street, finally, muffled in his ancient overcoat, shapeless felt hat pulled down over his baldhead, looking more like an energetic gnome than an eminent psychiatrist. His offices were nearby; I knew he had just left his last patient of the day. He was close to 80, but he still carried a full case load, still acted as director of a large foundation, still loved to escape to the golf course whenever he could.
What is your impression of the old men?
muffled紧裹着，ancient overcoat老旧的款式，年轻的时候穿的，老来发福还穿，所以用muffled
呢绒帽子，shapeless都没形了，拉下来盖住秃头
gnome精灵，身材矮小（土地公），energetic，表示这段描述positive，很有活力
offices：不止一间，可能有助手
carried：一般搭配具体的东西，这里搭配load，可以理解成，尽管他年近八十，但仍很忙碌
director：英语中的职位，某个部门的头，不像汉语划分那么清
golf course：高尔夫球场，course很多意思，注意搭配
4. By the time he came over and sat beside me, the waiter had brought his invariable bottle of ale. I had not seen him for several months, but he seemed as indestructible as ever. “Well, young man,” he said without preliminary, “what’s troubling you?”
第一句的时态，他还没来，waiter就已经把酒拿上来了
5. I had long since ceased to be surprised at his perceptiveness. So I proceeded to tell him, at some length, just what was bothering me. With a kind of melancholy pride, I tried to be very honest. I blamed no one else for my disappointment, only myself. I analyzed the whole thing, all the bad judgments, the false moves. I went on for perhaps 15 minutes, while the old man sipped his ale in silence.
melancholy pride：两个互相矛盾的词，形成某种特定的效果，像bitter sweet；这两个词分别指什么？melancholy是忧愁，pride分歧
  • Apparently the author was still proud of his "project of considerable importance", though he was sad because of several miscalculations on his part
  • 因为自己的honesty而pride，blame no one else, only myself
perceive：通过观察获得深刻的认识；conceive把五感获得的认识总结获得的认识
at some length：有长度，细节
6. When I finished, he put down his glass. “Come on,” he said, “Let’s go back to my office.”
去office，略过，直到第十段
7. “Your office? Did you forget something?”
8. “No,” he said mildly. “I want your reaction to something. That’s all.”
9. A chill rain was beginning to fall outside, but his office was warm and comfortable and familiar: book-lined walls, long leather couch, signed photograph of Sigmund Freud, tape recorder by the window. His secretary had gone home. We were alone.
10. The Old Man took a tape from a flat cardboard box and fitted it onto the machine. “On this tape,” he said, “are three short recordings made by three persons who came to me for help. They are not identified, of course. I want you to listen to the recordings and see if you can pick out the two-word phrase that is the common denominator in all three cases.” He smiled. “Don’t look so puzzled. I have my reasons.”
听三个人的磁带，找其中的共同点
11. What the owners of the voices on the tape had in common, it seemed to me, was unhappiness. The man who spoke first evidently had suffered some kind of business loss of failure; he berated himself for not having worked harder, for not having looked ahead. The woman who spoke next had never married because of a sense obligation to her widowed mother, she recalled bitterly all the marital chances she had let go by. The third voice belonged to a mother whose teen-age son was in trouble with the police; she blamed herself endlessly.
berated for not having：早先没有更加努力
谓语，berate，recall bitterly，blamed endlessly
12. The Old Man switched of the machine and leaned back in his chair. “Six times in those recordings a phrase is used that’s full of subtle poison. Did you spot it? No? Well, perhaps that’s because you used it three times yourself down in the restaurant a little while ago.” He picked up the box that had held the tape and tossed it over to me. “There they are, right on the label. The two saddest words in any language.”
spot：口语化，发现了没有
toss it over：over，表明两个人有一定的空间距离
There they are：倒装，改变句子结构，强调
13. I looked down. Printed neatly in red ink were the two words: if only.
14. “You’d be amazed,” said the old man, “if you knew how many thousands of times I’ve sat in this chair and listened to woeful sentences beginning with those two words. ‘if only.’ They say to me, ‘I had done it differently or not dine it at all. If only I hadn’t lost my temper, said the cruel thing, made the dishonest move, told that foolish lie. If only I had been wiser, or more unselfish, or more self-controlled,’ They go on and on until I stop them. Sometimes I make them listen to recordings you just heard ‘if only.’ I say to them, ‘you’d stop saying if only, we might begin to get somewhere!”
15. The Old Man stretched out his legs. “The trouble with ‘if only’ he said, “is that it doesn’t change anything. It keeps the person facing the wrong way --- backward instead of forward. It wastes time. In the end, if you let it become a habit, it can become a real roadblock, an excuse for not trying any more.”
work out：out做出来
16. “Now take your own case: your plans didn’t work out. Why? Because you made certain mistake. Well, that’s all right: everyone makes mistakes. Mistakes are what we learn from. But when you were telling me about them, lamenting this, regretting that, you weren’t really learning from them.”
that's all right: everyone：冒号表解释
17. “How do you know?” I said, a bit defensively.
defensively：辩护，有点不服气
18. “Because,” said the old man, “you never got out of the past tense. Not only did you mention the future. And in a way — be honest, now! — you were enjoying it. There’s a perverse streak in all of us that makes us like to hash over old mistakes. After all, when you relate the story of some disaster or disappointment that has happened to you, you’re still the chief character, still in the center of the stage.”
past sense：英语中的过去式，翻译的时候可以译成过去
心理学解析，只提过去，不讲将来，enjoying it
in a way—be honest, now!—you were插入语，大部分情况下，对说话内容的评价；now和were强烈对比；某种意义上，你这就是在，该清醒了；be honest表达这是客观事实的意思，所以从建议、从坦白都可以
19. I shook my head ruefully. “Well, what’s the remedy?”
shook my head：认识到自己的问题
remedy：解药是什么
20. “Shift the focus,” said the old man promptly.” Change the key words and substitute a phrase that supplies lift instead of creating drag.”
Change and substitute，and表示接踵而来
21. “Do you have such a phrase to recommend?”
22. “Certainly. Strike out the words’ if only’ substitute the phrase ‘next time.’”
23. “Next time?”
24. “That’s right. I’ve seen it work minor miracles right here in this room. As long as a patient keeps saying’ if only,’ to me, he’s in trouble. But when he looks me in the eye and says’ ‘next time.’ I know he’s on his way to overcoming his problem. It means he has decided to apply the lessons he has learned from his experience, however grim or painful it may have been. It means he’s going to push aside the roadblock of regret, move forward, take action, resume living. Try it yourself. You’ll see.”
标点，regret, move forward, take action, resume living. 为什么不用and，能否替换？作为连词，and表示顺序性、关联。逗号隔开，表明四个都是整体
25. My old friend stopped speaking outside, I could hear the rain whispering against the windowpane. I tried sliding one phrase out of my mind and replacing it with the other. It was fanciful, of course, but course, but I could hear the new words lock into place with an audible click….
26. The Old Man stood up a bit stiffly. “Well, class dismissed. It has been good to see you, young man. Always is. Now, if you will help me find a taxi, I probably should be getting on home.”
if you will条件句里还用will，为什么？will除了表示将来，还表示意愿，如果你愿意帮我叫taxi情态动词
should be getting on还是should get home，有什么区别？动身回家，should+进行体，表示态度，积极反应，我很愉快就可以回家了
27. We came out of the building into the rainy night. I stopped a cruising cab and ran toward it, but another pedestrian was quicker.
cruising cab，本义是巡逻，这里译为招徕乘客
28. “My, my,” said the Old Man slyly. “If only we had come down ten seconds sooner, we’d have caught that cab, wouldn’t we?”
My my瞧瞧瞧瞧，感叹词
29. I laughed and picked up the cue. “Next time I’ll run faster.”
30. “That’s it,” cried the Old Man, pulling his absurd hat down around his ears. “That’s it exactly.”
31. Another taxi slowed. I opened the door for him. He smiled and waved as it moved away. I never saw him again. A month later, he died of a sudden heart attack, in full stride, so to speak.
32. More than a year has passed since that rainy afternoon in Manhattan. But to this day, whenever I find myself thinking “if only”, I change it to “next time”. Then I wait for that almost-perceptible mental click. And when I hear it, I think of the Old Man.
33. A small fragment of immortality, to be sure. But it’s the kind he would have wanted.
翻译：这个小的语言片段总会改变我们的人生
首尾呼应
Theme: positive thinking

Notes

  • the sudden flash of insight
  • chewing the bitter cud of hindsight
  • -ible/-able，productive，构词方法
  • melancholy pride
  • substitute和replace，意义上大致相同，但是substitute可以构成名词，但是replace不行
  • click象声词；perceptible，可听到的、可看到的，这些都是perceptible
